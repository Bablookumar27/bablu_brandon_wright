# Memory: Brandon Wright

## Personal Profile

Brandon is a senior in the BSN program at Northfield College of Health Sciences and a competitive cross-country ski racer with the Twin Cities Nordic Ski Club. He graduated from Evergreen Valley High School in Edina, MN in 2022 and started the BSN program that fall. Expected graduation May 8, 2027.

His character runs on engineered efficiency. The 4:45 AM alarm, the color-coded calendar, the Sunday meal preps stacked through Wednesday, all of it is the architecture he built around a brain that does not rest when something is undone. The days it does not work, he skis until it does.

He is warm in the way that does not announce itself. He drives friends to clinicals in snowstorms before he tells them they look stressed. He leaves cinnamon rolls on the counter and refuses to take credit. People who do not know him well sometimes read the reserve as coldness; he finds it funny and never corrects them.

He is competitive in a way he is still negotiating. The junior racer who finished on state-podium boards is still in there; the senior nursing student who chose a career over an Olympic dream runs the calendar. The compromise has held since freshman year. The fourth-place finishes by less than a second he replays for a week.

His humor is dry enough that most people miss it. He delivers a one-liner with the same flat composure he uses to take a vitals chart, and walks away before the laugh lands. Hannah catches it first. Professor Chen caught it once and started taking him more seriously after.

Cultural identity is Scandinavian-American by way of the Midwest. The father's side carries Norwegian and Swedish from three generations of small-town Minnesota; the Lindgren line braids the same thread. None of it is loud, and he likes it that way. Strawberry jam is always Bonne Maman or Grandma Ingrid's; the Christmas table is rice pudding, lefse, and the princess cake he learned at thirteen.

Minnesota itself is the deeper culture. He grew up on the trails he now races on. He has been to other states for races, to Duluth for Nils, to the Boundary Waters with the family, and he comes home without missing anything.

Philosophy: the way you do small things is the way you do everything. Effort is its own ethic, not a performance. Grief lives in the body and gets out the same way; he has skied through most of the harder weeks of college without naming what he was skiing through. He believes his parents and grandparents deserve more than gratitude. The May 2027 graduation is, in his head, a payment.

He has high-functioning anxiety that he manages through structure. Perfectionism is the engine and the threat. He has gotten better at telling himself the system has slack. He has not gotten so good at it that the 4:45 AM alarm feels optional.
